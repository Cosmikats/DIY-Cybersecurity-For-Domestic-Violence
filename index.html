DIY Cybersecurity for Domestic Violence

A resource of resistance and compassion by HACK*BLOSSOM.

Why Cybersecurity?

Privacy is the freedom to make personal decisions without harm. Who will I text? Where will I hang out with friends? Do I check my bank account today? You might feel your partner is exploiting technology to control or hurt you, violating your privacy. Abuse isn't just physical. Being intimidated by invasive phone-calls, stalked by location-sharing apps, humiliated on social media, forced to share sensitive texts and passwords: these are all deeply hurtful experiences. They're no less harmful because they're digital. It's so easy for us to feel scared, lonely, or ashamed when we're treated this way by someone we care about.

DIY Cybersecurity resists control by asserting your privacy online. When you're threatened by your partner, physically or digitally, you can always reach out to a domestic violence professional. But what do you do when your partner is sending dozens of unwanted texts a day, or sharing sexual photos of you? Our Threat Scenarios explore, in-depth, how to resist coercive and controlling behaviors online. Each guide is accessible and empathetic: you're not obliged to follow any of our suggestions if you don't feel they're right for you. We also have Defense Strategies for proactively protecting your digital life.

We hope you find strength and security. You deserve it.
