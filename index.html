My partner is monitoring my computer or cell phone activity.

By monitoring your electronic devices with or without your consent, a controlling partner can create feelings of fear, anxiety, and helplessness. You have the right to decide how you use your electronics.

  • Read more

My partner has unwanted access to my online accounts.

Many controlling partners say that if you have "nothing to hide", you should be okay sharing emails, social media, or bank accounts. That's not their decision to make.

  • Read more

My sex life is being used against me.

Sexts, nudes, and pornograhy are ubiquitous in modern relationships. When a partner tries weaponize sexuality, you can resist.

Even after a relationship has ended, an abusive partner may attempt to harm you through online harassment. You can take action to protect yourself.
