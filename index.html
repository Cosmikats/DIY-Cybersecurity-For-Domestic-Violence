Why Cybersecurity?

Privacy is the freedom to make personal decisions without coercion. Who will I text? What videos will I watch? Do I check my bank account today? Online, these decisions feel small. But they guide your friendships, your creativity, and everything else that digitally connects you to the world. It's increasingly common for controlling or abusive partners to exploit these technologies to exert control. Coercive behavior isn't just physical: many partners inflict emotional harm through intimidation, isolation, manipulation, degradation, and surveillance. It's so easy to be anxious, afraid, hopeless, or lonely when you're treated this way.

DIY Cybersecurity resists control by asserting your privacy online. When you feel actively threatened by your partner, physically or digitally, you can always reach out to a domestic violence professional in your area. But what do you do when your partner is sending you dozens of unwanted texts a day, or is threatening to share sexual photos of you? Our Threat Scenarios explore, in-depth, how to resist common cases of coercive control. Each guide emphasizes accessibility, empathy, and autonomy: you're not obliged to follow any of our suggestions if you don't feel they're right for you. We also provide Defense Strategies for proactively protecting your digital activity.

We hope you find strength and security. You deserve it.
